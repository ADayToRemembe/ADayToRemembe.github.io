## Overview

This project involves a full CFD analysis of a De Laval (converging-diverging) nozzle designed for a small-scale solid rocket motor. The goal was to characterize the shock structure and thrust efficiency across a range of chamber pressures.

## Geometry and Mesh

The nozzle geometry features:
- Throat diameter: 12 mm
- Exit-to-throat area ratio: $A_e/A_t = 4.2$
- Half-angle of diverging section: 15°

Meshing was performed in **snappyHexMesh** with boundary layer refinement near the wall ($y^+ < 1$).

## Governing Equations

The compressible RANS equations are solved in conservation form:

$$\frac{\partial \rho}{\partial t} + \nabla \cdot (\rho \mathbf{u}) = 0$$

$$\frac{\partial (\rho \mathbf{u})}{\partial t} + \nabla \cdot (\rho \mathbf{u} \otimes \mathbf{u}) = -\nabla p + \nabla \cdot \boldsymbol{\tau}$$

For the ideal gas equation of state: $p = \rho R T$

## Results

The simulation correctly predicts the oblique shock train at off-design conditions. At the design pressure ratio, the flow remains shock-free through the diverging section with an exit Mach number of $M_e = 2.76$.

Specific impulse predicted: $I_{sp} \approx 185$ s (vacuum conditions).
