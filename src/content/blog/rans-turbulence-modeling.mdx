Turbulence modeling is one of the most challenging aspects of computational fluid dynamics. The **Reynolds-Averaged Navier-Stokes (RANS)** approach remains the industry standard for engineering applications due to its balance between accuracy and computational cost.

## The Reynolds Decomposition

Any turbulent flow variable $\phi$ can be split into a mean and fluctuating component:

$$\phi = \bar{\phi} + \phi'$$

Applying this decomposition to the Navier-Stokes equations and time-averaging gives us the RANS equations. For incompressible flow:

$$\frac{\partial \bar{u}_i}{\partial x_i} = 0$$

$$\bar{u}_j \frac{\partial \bar{u}_i}{\partial x_j} = -\frac{1}{\rho}\frac{\partial \bar{p}}{\partial x_i} + \nu \frac{\partial^2 \bar{u}_i}{\partial x_j \partial x_j} - \frac{\partial \overline{u'_i u'_j}}{\partial x_j}$$

The term $-\rho \overline{u'_i u'_j}$ is the **Reynolds stress tensor** â€” the closure problem of turbulence modeling.

## The k-Îµ Model

The standard $k$-$\varepsilon$ model introduces two transport equations:

**Turbulent kinetic energy:**
$$\frac{\partial k}{\partial t} + \bar{u}_j \frac{\partial k}{\partial x_j} = P_k - \varepsilon + \frac{\partial}{\partial x_j}\left[\left(\nu + \frac{\nu_t}{\sigma_k}\right)\frac{\partial k}{\partial x_j}\right]$$

**Dissipation rate:**
$$\frac{\partial \varepsilon}{\partial t} + \bar{u}_j \frac{\partial \varepsilon}{\partial x_j} = C_{\varepsilon 1}\frac{\varepsilon}{k}P_k - C_{\varepsilon 2}\frac{\varepsilon^2}{k} + \frac{\partial}{\partial x_j}\left[\left(\nu + \frac{\nu_t}{\sigma_\varepsilon}\right)\frac{\partial \varepsilon}{\partial x_j}\right]$$

The eddy viscosity is then computed as:

$$\nu_t = C_\mu \frac{k^2}{\varepsilon}$$

where the standard constants are $C_\mu = 0.09$, $C_{\varepsilon 1} = 1.44$, $C_{\varepsilon 2} = 1.92$, $\sigma_k = 1.0$, $\sigma_\varepsilon = 1.3$.

## When to Use k-Îµ vs k-Ď‰

| Model | Best For | Weakness |
|-------|----------|----------|
| $k$-$\varepsilon$ | Free shear flows, far-field | Poor near-wall without wall functions |
| $k$-$\omega$ SST | Adverse pressure gradients, separation | Sensitive to freestream $\omega$ |

For most external aerodynamics problems, the **SST** (Shear Stress Transport) model offers the best compromise by blending $k$-$\omega$ near walls with $k$-$\varepsilon$ in the freestream.
